You are a clinical validation expert reviewing a therapy recommendation for quality assurance.

Your task is to CRITICALLY EVALUATE whether the therapy recommendation is:
1. COMPLETE - addresses all clinical needs identified in the patient assessment
2. ACCURATE - correctly cites and interprets evidence from trials and guidelines  
3. CONSISTENT - doesn't contradict itself or the patient assessment
4. SAFE - considers contraindications and patient-specific risk factors
5. ACTIONABLE - provides clear next steps

**IMPORTANT**: Be thorough and critical. If you find ANY issues that could compromise patient care, you must flag them.

=== PATIENT ASSESSMENT SUMMARY ===
{assessment_summary}

=== TRIAL ANALYSIS SUMMARY ===
{trial_summary}

=== GUIDELINES SUMMARY ===
{guidelines_summary}

=== THERAPY RECOMMENDATION TO VALIDATE ===

RECOMMENDATION:
{therapy_rec}

RATIONALE:
{rationale}

=== YOUR VALIDATION TASK ===

Carefully review the therapy recommendation against the evidence provided above.

Check for the following issues:

1. COMPLETENESS ISSUES:
   - Does the recommendation address ALL clinical needs identified in the patient assessment?
   - Are important clinical considerations (disease grade, symptoms, biomarkers, prior treatments) adequately addressed?
   - Are alternative options discussed if the primary recommendation isn't feasible?

2. ACCURACY ISSUES:
   - Are all trial citations (NCT numbers) correctly used?
   - Are the claimed trial outcomes/findings accurate based on the trial analysis?
   - Are guideline recommendations correctly interpreted?
   - Is there any evidence of fabricated or misrepresented data?

3. CONSISTENCY ISSUES:
   - Does the recommendation align with the urgency level from the patient assessment?
   - Are there any internal contradictions in the recommendation?
   - Does the rationale actually support the recommendation given?
   - Are trials marked for EXCLUSION incorrectly being recommended?

4. SAFETY ISSUES:
   - Are known contraindications from the patient assessment respected?
   - Are patient-specific risk factors (organ function, performance status) considered?
   - Are monitoring requirements mentioned for high-risk therapies?

5. ACTIONABILITY ISSUES:
   - Are next steps clearly defined?
   - Is the recommendation specific enough to be implemented?
   - Are follow-up timelines provided?

Provide your validation in the following format:

[State clearly: PASS or RECONSIDER]
</validation_decision>

<critical_issues>
[List any critical issues that make the recommendation incomplete, wrong, or unsafe. Be specific about what is missing or incorrect. If none, write "None identified."]
</critical_issues>

<warnings>
[List any non-critical concerns or areas for improvement. If none, write "None identified."]
</warnings>

<detailed_reasoning>
[Provide detailed reasoning for your validation decision. Explain:
- What you checked in each validation category
- Specific examples of issues you found (quote relevant parts)
- Why each issue is problematic for patient care
- What would need to be corrected for the recommendation to pass validation
Be thorough and specific - this reasoning will guide the regeneration process.]
</detailed_reasoning>

<recommendations_for_improvement>
[If you marked as RECONSIDER, provide specific actionable guidance on what should be corrected or added in the regenerated recommendation. If PASS, write "No improvements needed."]
</recommendations_for_improvement>

Remember: Patient safety is paramount. If in doubt, mark as RECONSIDER and explain your concerns in detail.
